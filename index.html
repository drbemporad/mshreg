MCA § 53-21-1 — Treatment of the Seriously Mentally Ill
§ 53-21-101
Purpose
MCA 2023 · Ch. 466 L. 1975; amd. Ch. 490 L. 1997

The purpose of this part is to:

(1)Secure for each person who may be suffering from a mental disorder and requiring commitment the care and treatment suited to the needs of the person and to ensure that the care and treatment are skillfully and humanely administered with full respect for the person's dignity and personal integrity;

(2)Accomplish this goal whenever possible in a community-based setting;

(3)Accomplish this goal in an institutionalized setting only when less restrictive alternatives are unavailable or inadequate and only when a person is suffering from a mental disorder and requires commitment; and

(4)Ensure that due process of law is accorded any person coming under the provisions of this part.

§ 53-21-102
Definitions
MCA 2023 · Last amended Ch. 713 L. 2023

As used in this chapter, the following definitions apply:

(1)"Abuse" means any willful, negligent, or reckless mental, physical, sexual, or verbal mistreatment or maltreatment or misappropriation of personal property of any person receiving treatment in a mental health facility that insults the psychosocial, physical, or sexual integrity of any person receiving treatment in a mental health facility.

(2)"Behavioral health inpatient facility" means a facility or a distinct part of a facility of 16 beds or less licensed by the department that is capable of providing secure, inpatient psychiatric services, including services to persons with mental illness and co-occurring chemical dependency.

(3)"Board" or "mental disabilities board of visitors" means the mental disabilities board of visitors created by 2-15-211.

(4)"Commitment" means an order by a court requiring an individual to receive treatment for a mental disorder.

(5)"Court" means any district court of the state of Montana.

(6)"Department" means the department of public health and human services provided for in 2-15-2201.

(7)"Emergency situation" means: (a) a situation in which any person is in imminent danger of death or bodily harm from the activity of a person who appears to be suffering from a mental disorder and appears to require commitment; or (b) a situation in which any person who appears to be suffering from a mental disorder and appears to require commitment is substantially unable to provide for the person's own basic needs of food, clothing, shelter, health, or safety.

(8)"Friend of respondent" means any person willing and able to assist a person suffering from a mental disorder and requiring commitment in dealing with legal proceedings, including consultation with legal counsel and others.

(9)(a)"Mental disorder" means any organic, mental, or emotional impairment that has substantial adverse effects on an individual's cognitive or volitional functions. (b) The term does not include: (i) addiction to drugs or alcohol; (ii) drug or alcohol intoxication; (iii) intellectual disability; or (iv) epilepsy. (c) A mental disorder may co-occur with addiction or chemical dependency.

(10)"Mental health facility" or "facility" means the state hospital, the Montana mental health nursing care center, or a hospital, a behavioral health inpatient facility, a mental health center, a residential treatment facility, or a residential treatment center licensed or certified by the department that provides treatment to children or adults with a mental disorder. A correctional institution or facility or jail is not a mental health facility within the meaning of this part.

(11)"Mental health professional" means: (a) a certified professional person; (b) a physician licensed under Title 37, chapter 3; (c) a clinical professional counselor licensed under Title 37, chapter 39; (d) a psychologist licensed under Title 37, chapter 17; (e) a clinical social worker licensed under Title 37, chapter 39; (f) an APRN with a clinical specialty in psychiatric mental health nursing; (g) a physician assistant licensed under Title 37, chapter 20, with a clinical specialty in psychiatric mental health; or (h) a marriage and family therapist licensed under Title 37, chapter 39.

(12)(a)"Neglect" means failure to provide for the biological and psychosocial needs of any person receiving treatment in a mental health facility, failure to report abuse, or failure to exercise supervisory responsibilities to protect patients from abuse and neglect. Includes: (i) deprivation of food, shelter, appropriate clothing, nursing care, or other services; (ii) failure to follow a prescribed plan of care; or (iii) failure to respond to a person in an emergency situation by indifference, carelessness, or intention.

(13)"Next of kin" includes but is not limited to spouse, parents, adult children, and adult brothers and sisters.

(14)"Patient" means a person committed by the court for treatment or who is voluntarily admitted for treatment for any period of time.

(15)"Peace officer" means any sheriff, deputy sheriff, marshal, police officer, or other peace officer.

(16)"Professional person" means: (a) a medical doctor; (b) an APRN with a clinical specialty in psychiatric mental health nursing; (c) a licensed psychologist; (d) a physician assistant licensed under Title 37, chapter 20, with a clinical specialty in psychiatric mental health; or (e) a person who has been certified, as provided for in 53-21-106, by the department.

(17)"Reasonable medical certainty" means reasonable certainty as judged by the standards of a professional person.

(18)"Respondent" means a person alleged in a petition filed pursuant to this part to be suffering from a mental disorder and requiring commitment.

(19)"State hospital" means the Montana state hospital.

§ 53-21-104
Powers and Duties of Mental Disabilities Board of Visitors
MCA 2023 · Last amended Ch. 602 L. 2003

(1)The board is an independent board of inquiry and review responsible to ensure that the treatment of all persons either voluntarily or involuntarily admitted to a mental facility in Montana is humane, consistent with established clinical and other professional standards, and meets the requirements set forth in this part.

(2)The board shall review all plans for experimental research involving persons admitted to a mental health facility to ensure each project is humane, not unduly hazardous, and complies with applicable federal and professional standards. Research may not commence without board approval.

(3)(a)The board shall inspect every mental health facility that provides treatment or evaluation pursuant to this part, with an annual inspection schedule. (b) The board's authority to inspect may not be waived or precluded by other treatment review, licensing, or accreditation requirements. The board may inspect any facility at any time. (d) The board shall produce a written report of each inspection, including specific improvement recommendations, within 30 calendar days of completion. (f) The facility shall provide a written response to the board's report within 30 calendar days of receipt, including for each recommendation: (i) a specific plan for implementation; or (ii) a specific rationale for why it cannot be implemented.

(4)(b)The board shall review all aspects of treatment including the use of behavior-control procedures—mechanical restraints, locked and unlocked seclusion or isolation, time out, or other physical control—to ensure such procedures are clinically justified, closely monitored, and implemented only when less restrictive measures have failed.

(6)The board shall employ and is responsible for full-time legal counsel at the state hospital to act on behalf of all patients. Counsel shall periodically interview every patient and examine the patient's files and records.

(9)The board shall report annually to the governor concerning the status of mental health facilities inspected and occurrences of involuntary medication administration.

§ 53-21-106
Certification of Professional Persons
MCA 2023 · Ch. 578 L. 1983; amd. Ch. 546 L. 1995

(1)The department shall certify professional persons as defined in 53-21-102 for the purpose of this part.

(2)The department, with reference to recognized national standards in the field of mental health, shall adopt standards and rules governing the certification of professional persons.

(3)Certification rules must address at minimum: (a) type of education including degrees; (b) type of experience or training; (c) continuing education necessary to maintain certification; (d) an examination instrument for proficiency and understanding of mental health laws, diagnosis, and treatment; (e) procedure for categorical certification; and (f) specific procedures for certification, recertification, and revocation.

§ 53-21-107
Abuse and Neglect — Prohibition, Reporting, Investigations
MCA 2023 · Ch. 344 L. 2001; amd. Ch. 776 L. 2023

(1)Any form of abuse or neglect of a person admitted to a mental health facility is prohibited.

(2)Each mental health facility shall publish policies and procedures for detecting, reporting, investigating, determining the validity of, and resolving allegations of abuse or neglect.

(3)Each allegation of abuse or neglect must be reported as follows: (a) any employee with knowledge shall immediately report to the professional person in charge; (b) the professional person in charge shall report by end of next business day in writing to the board; (c) when an allegation may constitute a criminal act, the professional person in charge shall immediately report to the appropriate law enforcement authority.

(5)Investigations must be initiated no later than the end of the next business day; immediately when there is a report of an alleged criminal act.

(8)Mental health facilities shall provide a copy of the written investigation report within 5 working days of completion to both the director of the department and to the board.

(9)(a)For each allegation involving the Montana state hospital, the director shall report: (i) within 5 working days of the incident, the details; and (ii) within 5 working days of completion of the investigation, the written record — to the state protection and advocacy program. The director may not redact any information provided.

§ 53-21-111
Voluntary Admission — Content of Admission Form — Requirements
MCA 2023 · Ch. 466 L. 1975; amd. Ch. 247 L. 1999

(1)(a)This part may not be construed to limit the right of a person to make voluntary application for admission at any time to a mental health facility. (b) The admission form must explain: (i) the process for requesting release; (ii) that the individual may be held involuntarily for up to 5 days after requesting release; and (iii) that the facility may request a court to involuntarily commit the applicant. (c) A statement of patient rights must be furnished within 12 hours of admission.

(2)An applicant wishing to voluntarily apply to the state hospital must first obtain certification from a professional person that the applicant is suffering from a mental disorder, and the professional person must obtain confirmation from the department that facilities in the mental health region are unable to provide adequate evaluation and treatment.

(3)Voluntary admission gives the facility the right to detain the applicant for no more than 5 days (excluding weekends and holidays) past the applicant's written request for release.

(4)A person may not be admitted unless: (a) approved by a professional person; (b) informed orally of admission form contents; (c) given a copy of the written application; and (d) admission otherwise complies with this section.

(5)A person voluntarily entering or remaining in a mental health facility shall enjoy all the rights secured to a person involuntarily committed.

§ 53-21-120
Detention — Least Restrictive Environment
MCA 2023

(1)A person detained pursuant to this part must be detained in the least restrictive environment required to protect the life and physical safety of the person detained or members of the public.

(2)Whenever possible, detention must be in a mental health facility in the county of residence. If a jury trial is demanded and cannot be held within 7 days, the individual may be sent to the state hospital or a behavioral health inpatient facility until trial, which must be held within 30 days.

(3)A person may not be detained pursuant to this part in a jail or other correctional facility.

§ 53-21-121
Petition for Commitment — Contents and Notice
MCA 2023

(1)The county attorney, upon the written request of any person having direct knowledge of the facts, or any person with direct knowledge of the facts, may file a petition with the court alleging that there is a person within the county who is suffering from a mental disorder and who requires commitment pursuant to this chapter.

The petition must include: the respondent's name; the purported facts supporting the allegation of mental disorder (including any professional person's report); a statement of the disposition sought; and supporting information. All named parties must receive notice.

§ 53-21-126
Trial or Hearing on Petition — Commitment Criteria
MCA 2023

The court may order commitment only upon finding clear and convincing evidence that the respondent is suffering from a mental disorder and requires commitment. Commitment to the state hospital requires that community-based alternatives are unavailable or inadequate.

Note (§ 53-21-127): If the court relies solely upon the criterion of predictability (§ 53-21-126(1)(d)), the court may require commitment only to a community facility or program, and may not require commitment at the state hospital, a behavioral health inpatient facility, or the Montana mental health nursing care center.
§ 53-21-129
Emergency Situation — Petition — Detention
MCA 2023 · Ch. 466 L. 1975; amd. Ch. 308 L. 2013

(1)When an emergency situation exists, a peace officer may take any person who appears to have a mental disorder and to present an imminent danger of death or bodily harm into custody only for sufficient time to contact a professional person for emergency evaluation. If possible, a professional person should be called prior to taking the person into custody.

(2)If the professional person agrees that an emergency situation exists, the person may be detained.

(4)Before a person may be transferred to the state hospital or to a behavioral health inpatient facility under this section, the receiving facility must be notified prior to transfer and shall state whether a bed is available. If the professional person determines a behavioral health inpatient facility is appropriate and a bed is available, the county attorney shall direct the person to that facility.

MCA § 53-21-6 — Montana State Hospital
§ 53-21-601
Location and Primary Function of Hospital
MCA 2025 · En. Ch. 199 L. 1965; amd. Ch. 247 L. 1999

(1)The facility providing mental health care services at Warm Springs, Montana, is the Montana state hospital and as its primary function provides care and treatment of mentally ill persons.

(2)(a)The Montana state hospital is a mental health facility, as defined in § 53-21-102, of the department of public health and human services for the care and treatment of mentally ill persons.

(2)(b)The role of the Montana state hospital is to provide intensive inpatient psychiatric services, including those services necessary for transition to community care, as components in a comprehensive continuum of publicly and privately provided programs that emphasize treatment in the least restrictive environment.

(2)(c)Mission: The mission of the Montana state hospital is to stabilize persons with severe mental illness and to return them to the community as soon as possible if adequate community-based support services are available.

(3)The department shall adopt rules to manage the state hospital patient population in a manner that will: ensure emergency access to services; protect public and individual safety; provide active treatment; implement effective discharge planning; and ensure access to appropriate community-based services.

ARM 37.106.3 — Construction & Minimum Standards for All Health Care Facilities
Scope: ARM 37.106, Subchapter 3 applies to all licensed health care facilities in Montana, including the Montana State Hospital. Subchapter-specific rules (e.g., ARM 37.106.4 for hospitals) supplement — and may override — these general standards where conflicts exist.
ARM 37.106.301
Definitions

Key terms applicable throughout Subchapter 3. Definitions align with those in 50-5-101, MCA (the Health Care Facility Licensing Act) and include:

Health care facility — as defined in 50-5-101, MCA; encompasses hospitals, nursing homes, mental health facilities, and other licensed care settings.

Department — Montana Department of Public Health and Human Services (DPHHS), Office of Inspector General, Licensure Bureau.

Eff. 11/15/2013 · AUTH: 50-5-103, MCA

ARM 37.106.302
Minimum Standards of Construction: General Requirements

All new construction, alterations, or additions to health care facilities must comply with applicable state and local building codes and fire safety standards. Construction plans must be reviewed and approved by the department prior to commencement.

Requirements include: fire-resistive construction ratings, adequate electrical systems, plumbing and ventilation meeting applicable codes, accessibility for persons with disabilities (ADA compliance), and structural integrity standards.

Eff. 11/15/2013 · AUTH: 50-5-103, MCA

ARM 37.106.306
Submission of Plans and Specifications — New Construction, Alteration, or Addition

A health care facility proposing new construction, alteration, or addition shall submit plans and specifications to the department for review before construction begins. Plans must be prepared by a licensed architect or engineer as required by applicable state law. The department shall review plans for compliance with these rules and applicable construction standards.

Eff. 11/15/2013 · AUTH: 50-5-103, MCA

ARM 37.106.310
Licensing: Procedure for Obtaining, Issuing, and Renewing a License

Every health care facility operating in Montana must be licensed by the department. The licensing process requires:

(a)Submission of a completed license application with required fees to the DPHHS Office of Inspector General, Licensure Bureau.

(b)Compliance with all applicable licensure rules and construction standards.

(c)Inspection by the department prior to initial licensure and at renewal.

(d)Annual or periodic renewal as established by rule. A license is not subject to sale, assignment, or transfer.

(e)Notice to the department of any changes in facility location, use, or number of beds; such changes require written departmental approval.

Eff. 11/15/2013 · AUTH: 50-5-103, MCA; IMP: 50-5-111, 50-5-201, 50-5-202, 50-5-203, 50-5-204, MCA

ARM 37.106.311
Minimum Standards for All Health Care Facilities: Administration

Each health care facility must maintain adequate administrative structure including:

A governing body or authority legally responsible for the facility's operation.

A designated administrator or director responsible for day-to-day management.

Written policies and procedures governing all aspects of facility operation.

An organizational chart accurately reflecting current lines of authority and administration.

Documentation of ownership and management structure filed with the department.

Eff. 11/15/2013 · AUTH: 50-5-103, MCA

ARM 37.106.314
Minimum Standards for All Health Care Facilities: Medical Records

(1)A health care facility must initiate and maintain a safe, secure, and confidential medical record for each patient, resident, or client.

(2)A health care facility, excluding a hospital, shall retain a patient's, resident's, or client's medical records for no less than six years following the date of the patient's, resident's, or client's discharge or death, or upon the closure of the facility. (Hospitals are subject to separate retention requirements under ARM 37.106.402.)

ARM 37.106.3 — Patient Rights (General)
Minimum Standards for All Health Care Facilities: Patient/Resident Rights

All licensed health care facilities must develop and maintain a patient/client rights policy that supports and protects applicable state and federal rights, including:

The right to be informed of one's rights prior to or upon admission.

The right to be treated with dignity and respect.

Freedom from abuse, neglect, and exploitation.

The right to participate in care planning.

The right to privacy and confidentiality of records.

The right to file grievances without fear of retaliation.

For psychiatric facilities, these requirements supplement the statutory patient rights under MCA §§ 53-21-141 through 53-21-152.

ARM 37.106.4 — Minimum Standards for a Hospital
Applicability: ARM 37.106, Subchapter 4 establishes hospital-specific minimum standards. These rules apply to the Montana State Hospital as a licensed hospital under Title 50, Chapter 5, MCA, and supplement the general facility standards in ARM 37.106.3.
ARM 37.106.401
Minimum Standards for a Hospital: General Requirements

A hospital must meet the following general requirements to maintain its license from the department:

Governing Body: A hospital must have a governing body with legal responsibility for the operation of the hospital, including establishment of policy, maintenance of quality care, and appointment of a qualified administrator and medical staff.

Medical Staff: The hospital must have an organized medical staff that is responsible for the quality of medical care provided. The medical staff must operate under bylaws approved by the governing body.

Nursing Services: Adequate nursing staff, organized under a director of nursing, must be available 24 hours per day to meet patients' nursing care needs.

Dietary Services: The hospital must provide or arrange for dietary services that meet patients' nutritional needs and any special dietary requirements under medical supervision.

Pharmacy Services: Pharmaceutical services must be available to patients, with supervision by a licensed pharmacist. Drug storage and dispensing must comply with applicable laws and regulations.

Infection Control: The hospital must maintain a formal infection control program, with designated responsibility, surveillance, and documented interventions to reduce hospital-acquired infections.

Quality Assurance: A hospital must operate a quality assurance program with regular review of the quality and appropriateness of care provided. Findings must be documented and corrective actions taken as warranted.

Staffing: The hospital must employ or have available sufficient personnel qualified to perform all necessary functions, with staff credentials verified and maintained on file.

NEW 1980; AMD 1996, 1999, 2003, 2022 · Eff. 9/24/2022 · AUTH: 50-5-103, 50-5-404, MCA; IMP: 50-5-103, 50-5-204, 50-5-404, MCA

ARM 37.106.402
Minimum Standards for a Hospital: Medical Records

Medical records in a hospital setting must comply with the following requirements:

A complete and accurate medical record must be maintained for each patient, containing: identification data; admitting diagnosis; history and physical; physician orders; progress notes; nursing notes; reports of clinical observations and procedures; laboratory and other diagnostic reports; and discharge summary.

Medical records must be completed within 30 days of patient discharge. Delinquent record policies must be enforced.

Records must be retained in accordance with applicable law. Hospital medical records must be retained for a minimum period consistent with state and federal requirements (generally, the longer of 10 years from discharge for adults, or until the patient's 19th birthday for minors).

Records must be stored securely to prevent unauthorized access, damage, or destruction. Electronic medical records must comply with HIPAA and applicable state privacy requirements.

Eff. 6/29/1990 · AUTH: 50-5-103, 50-5-404, MCA; IMP: 50-5-103, 50-5-106, 50-5-404, MCA

ARM 37.106.403
Minimum Standards for a Hospital: Hospital Records

Each hospital must maintain statistical and administrative records including patient census data, admissions, discharges, deaths, and transfers. These records must be available for departmental inspection. The hospital must submit statistical reports to the department as required by rule and statute.

Eff. 6/13/1980 · AUTH: 50-5-103, 50-5-404, MCA

ARM 37.106.404
Minimum Standards for a Hospital: Laboratories

Hospitals must provide or arrange for adequate laboratory services to meet patients' clinical needs. Any laboratory operated by a hospital must hold a valid Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments (CLIA) certificate from the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services. Laboratory services must be available 24 hours per day for emergency testing. Results must be documented promptly in the patient's medical record.

Eff. 6/13/1980 · AUTH: 50-5-103, 50-5-404, MCA

ARM 37.106.405
Minimum Standards for a Hospital: Organ Donation Requests and Protocols

Each hospital must have written protocols for identifying and referring potential organ donors. The hospital must maintain a written agreement with an organ procurement organization (OPO) designated under federal law. Staff must be trained in implementing organ donation protocols. The hospital may not implement a policy that restricts the ability of appropriate individuals to consent to organ donation.

Eff. 10/16/1987 · AUTH: 50-5-103, 50-5-404, MCA

ARM 37.106.410
Minimum Standards for a Hospital: Coronary Care Unit

Hospitals operating a coronary care unit (CCU) must meet minimum standards including continuous cardiac monitoring capability, advanced life support equipment, and staff with appropriate training in cardiac care. This rule applies to hospitals providing CCU services; psychiatric hospitals without CCU services need not comply with CCU-specific provisions.

Eff. 6/13/1980 · AUTH: 50-5-103, 50-5-404, MCA

ARM 37.106.411 et seq.
Minimum Standards for a Hospital: Intensive Care and Specialty Units

Hospitals operating intensive care units (ICUs) or other specialty care units must comply with additional equipment, staffing, and monitoring standards appropriate to the services provided. These requirements are specific to hospitals offering such services and do not apply where those services are not provided.

Psychiatric Hospital Note: The Montana State Hospital, as a psychiatric facility, is primarily subject to the general hospital standards (ARM 37.106.401–405) and applicable behavioral health licensing requirements. Specialty unit standards apply only to services actually provided at MSH.
ARM 37.106.4 — Psychiatric Hospital Application
Application of Hospital Standards to Psychiatric Facilities

The Montana State Hospital operates as a licensed hospital under ARM 37.106.4. As a psychiatric facility, it is subject to all applicable hospital minimum standards, with the following considerations:

Governing body and administration requirements apply in full (ARM 37.106.401).

Medical staff must include psychiatrists and other physicians; organized under bylaws with peer review and credentialing functions.

Medical records must comply with ARM 37.106.402, with additional confidentiality protections under MCA § 53-21-166.

Nursing and psychiatric services — 24/7 nursing coverage required; mental health professional coverage required for evaluation, treatment planning, and crisis management.

Restraint and seclusion — policies must comply with both ARM 37.106.3 general standards and MCA § 53-21-146 (right to be free from physical restraint and isolation), including documentation and physician review requirements.

Quality assurance — psychiatric hospitals must include review of treatment outcomes, medication practices, restraint/seclusion use, and compliance with patient rights in their QA programs.

Discharge planning — consistent with MSH's statutory mission (§ 53-21-601), discharge planning toward community placement is a required component of treatment planning.

See also: ARM 37.106.3 (general facility standards); MCA §§ 53-21-101 et seq. (statutory patient rights and admission/discharge requirements)

ARM 37.106.806
License Application Process (General Licensure)

Any person, group, or corporation proposing to establish or operate a health care facility must submit a completed license application to the department. Key requirements:

Applications must be submitted on forms prescribed by the department, including documentation of ownership, corporate structure, and key personnel.

Background checks must be completed for all owners, operators, and key personnel as required.

Operating a health care facility without a current, valid license from the department is a violation of law and subject to prosecution and civil penalties under 50-5-111, 50-5-112, 50-5-113, MCA.

License renewal must be submitted prior to expiration. The department may inspect the facility at renewal.
